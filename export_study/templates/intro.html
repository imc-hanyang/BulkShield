This study aims to move away from post-punishment methods based on uniform criteria. Instead, it seeks to analyze user booking patterns to preemptively identify users with a high probability of mass refunds, thereby preventing seat hogging and ensuring fair seat access for all customers.

To this end, we aim to verify the validity of detection result interpretations and mass refund risk group criteria through validation by SRT practitioners, thereby securing the reliability of this research.

ü§ñ Expert Survey #1: Evaluation of Preemptive Mass Refund Risk Detection Result Interpretation

The research team has generated explanations for users classified as mass refund risk groups by the developed preemptive detection model, to facilitate understanding of the basis for these results and booking intentions.

In this step, we aim to evaluate whether the generated explanations help in understanding the user's booking/refund behavior and whether they can be used as reference material for determining management or action in practice.

üìù Expert Survey #2: Verification of Criteria for Mass Refund and Normal Groups

For the development of the preemptive mass refund detection model, the research team analyzed users' refund patterns to distinguish between suspected mass refund groups and normal groups.

In this step, we aim to verify whether these criteria are valid from an SRT practical perspective. We ask for your opinion on whether each case requires management or interpretation of booking intentions, to secure the reliability of model development.
